Ten Interesting Facts about NBA

Read time: 5 mintues

Here is some information about the NBA!

1. Longest Winning Streak: The Los Angeles Lakers hold the record for the longest winning streak in NBA history, with 33 consecutive victories during the 1971-72 season.

2. Wilt Chamberlain's 100-Point Game: Wilt Chamberlain is the only player in NBA history to score 100 points in a single game, achieving this feat on March 2, 1962, against the New York Knicks.

3. Shortest Player vs. Tallest Player: The shortest player in NBA history was Muggsy Bogues at 5’3”, while the tallest was Manute Bol at 7’7”. They even played against each other at one point!

4. The Boston Celtics’ Dominance: The Celtics dominated the 1950s and 1960s, winning 11 championships in 13 years, including an eight-year streak from 1959 to 1966 under legendary coach Red Auerbach.

5. Michael Jordan’s Retirement: Michael Jordan retired three times from the NBA. His first retirement came in 1993 after winning three championships. He briefly pursued a baseball career before returning in 1995, then retired again in 1999 and came back for a final stint with the Washington Wizards in 2001.

6. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar’s Scoring Record: For nearly 40 years, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar held the record for the most points in NBA history with 38,387, before LeBron James surpassed him in February 2023.

7. The First Slam Dunk Contest: The first-ever NBA Slam Dunk Contest was held in 1984 during the NBA All-Star Weekend, and it was won by Larry Nance of the Phoenix Suns.

8. First International Draft Pick: In 1984, the Dallas Mavericks selected Nigerian player Hakeem Olajuwon with the first overall pick, marking one of the earliest selections of an international player in the NBA Draft.

9. Highest-Scoring Game: The highest-scoring game in NBA history occurred on December 13, 1983, between the Detroit Pistons and the Denver Nuggets, with a combined score of 370 points (186-184). The Pistons won in triple overtime.

10. NBA’s Expansion into China: The NBA has a massive following in China, and the first NBA player from China was Wang Zhizhi, who debuted in 2001. Yao Ming, one of the most famous Chinese players, further expanded the league’s global reach. He was inducted into the Hall of Fame in 2016.
